How to read this plan

One base, five days, something for everyone

Each day runs on the same rhythm you asked for: a morning out, a slow break (14:00–17:00) back near the apartment, and an evening. Most days also have a late brothers' night. One of you keeps kosher and is also gluten-free; the other three eat anything. So each day the kosher tables are also the gluten-free-safe choice for that one person — lean on grilled meats, salads, hummus and shakshuka, and skip pita, falafel, couscous and matzo balls — while the three have free rein.

Before you go — the fine print that matters

Practical notes

Kosher + gluten-free — one traveller

Only one of you needs kosher, and that same person is also gluten-free, so their meals are at the kosher spots (Carmel, Hanna, Hummus Bar, Café Tel Aviv), choosing the naturally gluten-free dishes. Carmel and Hanna are glatt under Chabad / Orthodox supervision; all are Sun–Thu and closed Saturday. The other three eat wherever they like.

Tip: book Carmel ahead for a group.

Gluten-free — the safe picks

Because the gluten-free traveller also keeps kosher, the safe meals are the naturally gluten-free dishes at the kosher places: grilled meats, soups, salads, hummus and shakshuka. Avoid pita, falafel, couscous, breaded schnitzel, cholent (barley) and matzo balls, and ask the kitchen if unsure. The dedicated gluten-free restaurants in town aren't kosher, so they're not an option for that person.

A gentle, unhurried pace

The days are built for slow walking with plenty of stops to sit and rest. Pest is flat and the apartment is minutes from the kosher table on level streets; the river cruise is seated throughout, and the one hillier spot — Buda Castle — is reached by funicular, so there is no real climbing anywhere.

Book ahead

Parliament tour (slots sell out), the evening cruise, and a group table at Carmel. Ask the cruise operator for an easy, low-step boarding, and request a comfortable car for the transfer — including the ~02:45 airport run on the last night.

Live music & concerts

Big-name October shows aren't all announced this far out. Check eventim.hu and songkick.com closer to the date. For a reliable, easy evening, the Hungarian State Opera House (Andrássy út 22) and the Liszt Academy (Liszt Ferenc tér 8) are world-class and beautiful.

Money & transport

Prices are approximate, per person, ~€1 ≈ 395 HUF. The center is walkable; for everything else, Bolt is cheap and door-to-door (best with mobility). A 72-hour BKK travelcard (~€15) covers trams and metro if you'd rather ride.
