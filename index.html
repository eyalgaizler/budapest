Before you go — the fine print that matters

Practical notes

Kosher — who can share

The kosher restaurants (Carmel, Hanna, Hummus Bar, Café Tel Aviv) are the tables all four can share. Carmel and Hanna are glatt under Chabad / Orthodox supervision; all are Sun–Thu and closed Saturday.

Tip: book Carmel ahead for a group.

Gluten-free — safest bets

Dedicated 100% GF kitchens (Drop, NonGlutén, Bohémtanya) are the celiac-safe choices and double as great regular restaurants. Inside the kosher places, lean on grilled meats, soups and salads, and ask the kitchen — cholent (barley) and matzo balls contain gluten.

Accessibility

Pest is flat; the apartment is minutes from the kosher table on level streets. The Buda hill is solved by the funicular, and the river cruise is seated. Day 4 caution: Szentendre is cobbled and Visegrád is a hilltop — the coach does the driving, but the gentler Gödöllő alternative is there if needed.

Book ahead

Parliament tour (slots sell out), the Danube Bend group tour, the evening cruise, and a group table at Carmel. Confirm step-free boarding with the cruise operator and an accessible car with the transfer.

Live music & concerts

Big-name October shows aren't all announced this far out. Check eventim.hu and songkick.com closer to the date. For a reliable, accessible evening, the Hungarian State Opera House (Andrássy út 22) and the Liszt Academy (Liszt Ferenc tér 8) are world-class and beautiful.

Money & transport

Prices are approximate, per person, ~€1 ≈ 395 HUF. The center is walkable; for everything else, Bolt is cheap and door-to-door (best with mobility). A 72-hour BKK travelcard (~€15) covers trams and metro if you'd rather ride.
